The Problems

Isolation and Loneliness

According to AARP, nearly 35% of people aged 45 and older report feeling lonely. For veterans, the isolation can be even more profound due to unique experiences and the loss of camaraderie from their service days. Loneliness has been linked to numerous health problems, including depression, anxiety, cognitive decline, and increased mortality rates.

Lack of continuous stimulation

Many elderly individuals lack opportunities for mental and physical engagement. Activities that keep their minds engaged and bodies active are crucial in maintaining their quality of life.

Evening Fatigue and Need for Special Attention

As the day progresses, many elderly people experience increased fatigue, making evenings particularly challenging. They require more attention and specialized care during these hours. Vintage Veterans offers tailored evening programs and care schedules to ensure that our veterans receive the attention and support they need as the day winds down.

Healthcare Access

A study by the National Council on Aging found that 80% of older adults have at least one chronic disease, and 77% have at least two. Access to regular, quality healthcare is often limited, especially for those who are isolated.* Intervention: We provide assistance in accessing healthcare services, ensuring that our veterans receive the medical attention they need without unnecessary delays.
